A strong headline performance, but deeper structural shifts threaten long-term sustainability.

Profitability by Segment

Not all products contribute equally.

  • Gold Hospital: The undisputed profit engine of the portfolio.
  • Bronze & Silver: Operating at a net loss per policy.
  • Premium Extras: Subsidising the entry-level options.

The "Subsidy Squeeze"

The Structural Threat

  • Shrinking: High-margin Gold Hospital (-19.1%) and Premium Extras.
  • Growing: Loss-making Bronze (+10.1%) & Silver Hospital (+30.6%).

Conclusion: Profitable segments are shrinking rapidly while loss-making segments grow, squeezing future margins.

Membership Attrition

Losses are driven by external cancellations, not internal downgrades.

  • Gold: Members are abandoning HBF entirely, taking their profits with them.
  • Silver: Growth is fueled by new external joiners acquiring cheap cover.

The Impact of Risk Equalisation

RE transforms gross profits into net losses for lower tiers.

  • Gold: Receives massive RE receipts due to older, sicker demographics.
  • Bronze & Silver: Young, healthy demographics force HBF to pay heavily into the industry pool.

The Silver 0 Clinical Anomaly

A massive outlier: $4,218 net loss per policy

Silver 0 operates at an unsustainable 169.5% net loss ratio over the 2-year period, far worse than any other Silver product.

  • Driven by the Mental Health Waiver, allowing a once-per-lifetime bypass of the 2-month waiting period for psychiatric care.
  • The "Hit & Drop" Effect: Members upgrade to Silver 0 to avoid out-of-pocket excesses, claim thousands for in-patient care, and immediately downgrade post-discharge.

Disproportionate Impact: Despite making up only 0.92% of the hospital portfolio, Silver 0 single-handedly erodes 5% of the entire division's net profit.

The "Hit & Drop" Evidence

Over 2 years, a massive 1,655 policies entered this portfolio (which averages only 2,091 active policies).

  • 743 Full Cancellations: The vast majority didn't just downgrade—they fully completely exited HBF after claiming.
  • Unsustainable Churn: This specific product acts as a revolving door for psychiatric claims.

Strategic Interventions

  1. Gold Product Retention: Address external cancellations via loyalty benefits, premium service features, or age-based discounts.
  2. Reprice Bronze/Silver: Adjust premiums to reflect true post-RE costs, rather than just gross claims.
  3. Address Silver 0 Psychiatry: Review pricing, excess levels, or coordinate with clinical networks to stem the "Hit & Drop" bleeding.
